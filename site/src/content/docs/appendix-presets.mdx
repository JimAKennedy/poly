This appendix documents all fourteen factory presets shipped with Poly. Each preset is a starting point, not a destination — the parameter tables and experiment suggestions below are designed to help you understand what each preset does and where you might take it.

## 1. Four on the Floor

A straight four-on-the-floor kick anchored by a backbeat snare and steady hi-hats, with a polymetric ghost layer at E(7,16) that introduces subtle asymmetric motion beneath the grid.

<PolyPatch title="Four on the Floor" preset="Factory: Four on the Floor">

| Lane | Role | Steps | Hits | Rotation | Subdivision | Note | Velocity | Ghost | Swing |
|------|------|-------|------|----------|-------------|------|----------|-------|-------|
| 1 | Kick | 4 | 4 | 0 | 1/4 | 36 | 110 | 0 | 0 |
| 2 | Snare | 4 | 2 | 1 | 1/4 | 38 | 95 | 0 | 0 |
| 3 | Hi-hat | 16 | 8 | 0 | 1/16 | 42 | 70 | 35 | 0.15 |
| 4 | Ghost | 16 | 7 | 3 | 1/16 | 42 | 45 | 50 | 0.15 |

</PolyPatch>

**Macros:** Density 0.6, Complexity 0.3, Syncopation 0.2, Swing 0.3, Tension 0.4, Humanize 0.15

**Experiments:**
- Push the Swing macro to 0.5 for a house feel, or drop it to 0 for rigid techno.
- Increase Lane 4's hits from 7 to 9 and listen for how the ghost layer thickens.
- Set Lane 4's steps to 15 instead of 16 to create a true polymetric ghost pattern that drifts against the kick grid.

## 2. Polymetric Drift

Four lanes at prime-number cycle lengths — 3, 5, 7, and 11 steps. Because no two primes share a common factor, the combined pattern does not repeat for `lcm(3, 5, 7, 11) = 1155` steps. The groove is always recognisable but never literally repeating.

<PolyPatch title="Polymetric Drift" preset="Factory: Polymetric Drift">

| Lane | Role | Steps | Hits | Rotation | Subdivision | Note | Velocity | Ghost | Swing |
|------|------|-------|------|----------|-------------|------|----------|-------|-------|
| 1 | Kick pulse | 3 | 2 | 0 | 1/4 | 36 | 105 | 0 | 0 |
| 2 | Rim pattern | 5 | 3 | 1 | 1/8 | 37 | 80 | 30 | 0 |
| 3 | Tom accent | 7 | 4 | 0 | 1/8 | 47 | 85 | 40 | 0 |
| 4 | Hat shimmer | 11 | 7 | 2 | 1/8 | 42 | 60 | 45 | 0.10 |

</PolyPatch>

**Macros:** Density 0.5, Complexity 0.4, Syncopation 0.3, Swing 0.1, Tension 0.5, Humanize 0.20

**Experiments:**
- Automate the Density macro from 0.2 to 0.7 over 16 bars to hear the prime-ratio interactions emerge gradually.
- Try replacing Lane 1's steps with 4 (non-prime) and notice how the groove becomes more predictable.
- Increase Humanize to 0.4 for a loose, organic feel that makes the polymetric shifts less mechanical.

## 6. Afro-House Phrases

The phrase offsets ensure that at least two lanes are always active, preventing complete silence while maintaining the feeling of structural variation.

## 7. Reich Phasing

Two lanes run the identical pattern E(5,12), but Lane 2 applies drift, causing it to gradually shift phase relative to Lane 1. The third lane provides a sparse structural anchor. Over many bars, the two primary voices move from unison through various phase relationships and eventually (if drift is slow enough) back to unison — the essence of Steve Reich's phasing technique.

| 2 | Pattern B (drift) | 12 | 5 | 0 | 1/8 | 58 | 80 | 0 | +1 |
| 3 | Anchor pulse | 4 | 2 | 0 | 1/4 | 36 | 95 | 0 | 0 |

</PolyPatch>

**Macros:** Density 0.7, Complexity 0.2, Syncopation 0.1, Swing 0, Tension 0.3, Humanize 0.05

**Experiments:**
- Increase Lane 2's drift to +2 for faster phasing — the patterns separate and reconverge more quickly.
- Change the note assignments so Lanes 1 and 2 play different pitched percussion — the phasing becomes a melodic canon.
- Set Humanize to 0 for maximum precision, or push it to 0.3 to hear how looseness interacts with the phasing process.

## 9. Pocket Groove

A tight four-lane groove emphasising micro-timing offsets and subtle mutation. The kick and snare lock together with precise pocket placement, while the hat and ghost lanes apply controlled looseness. This preset demonstrates that polymetric interest can come from timing variation rather than cycle-length differences.

<PolyPatch title="Pocket Groove" preset="Factory: Pocket Groove">

## 10. Afrobeat 12/8

A compound-time groove built around a fixed timeline bell pattern — the 12/8 standard that organises West African ensemble music. The bell plays E(7,12) as a timeline (immune to macro modulation), while a four-on-the-floor kick anchors the downbeat. Shaker fills continuously at 12/12, and conga ghosts drift underneath with humanised timing.

- Try scene morphing from this preset to Pocket Groove — the compound-to-straight transition is a classic Afrobeat arrangement technique.

## 14. IDM Glitch

Irregular additive cells [3+2+5+3] with heavy mutation, erratic per-step micro-timing offsets, and high probability variation across five lanes. This preset is deliberately unstable — it uses mutation rates up to 0.4 and micro-timing swings of ±12ms to create patterns that never settle into a repeatable groove. The Complexity and Tension macros push the engine toward maximum rhythmic entropy.

Factory presets are starting points. Every parameter in every table above is a variable you can change. The presets demonstrate specific techniques — polymetric drift, phrase gating, kotekan interlocking, micro-timing pocketing — but their real purpose is to give you a working patch you can modify rather than a blank slate you have to build from scratch. Load a preset, listen to it for a few bars, then change one parameter at a time and listen to what shifts. That iterative process — load, listen, modify, listen — is how you develop an intuition for Poly's parameter space.
