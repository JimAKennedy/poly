## Install

Copy the `Poly.vst3` bundle to your system's VST3 plugin folder:

- **macOS:** `~/Library/Audio/Plug-Ins/VST3/`
- **Windows:** `C:\Program Files\Common Files\VST3\`

Restart your DAW so it rescans for new plugins.

## Load Poly

Add an instrument track with Poly as the instrument. In Cubase: **Project > Add Track > Instrument**, then select **Poly** from the plugin list.

Poly generates MIDI only — it produces no audio on its own. You will need a drum sound source to hear the output.

## Route MIDI to a Drum Instrument

You need a drum instrument (Groove Agent, Battery, Kontakt, or any GM-compatible drum plugin) to turn Poly's MIDI into sound.

- **Cubase:** Open the Inspector on the Poly track. Under **MIDI Sends**, enable a send and route it to your drum instrument's MIDI input.
- **Other DAWs:** Route Poly's MIDI output to a track hosting your drum instrument. The exact method varies — check your DAW's MIDI routing documentation.

## Pick a Preset

Click the preset dropdown in Poly's header bar. Try **Four on the Floor** for a straight groove or **Polymetric Drift** for evolving prime-number patterns. There are 14 factory presets covering styles from Afro-Cuban clave to IDM glitch.

## Press Play

Hit play in your DAW. You will see the lane phase indicators animate and hear the groove through your drum instrument. The output is deterministic — the same settings always produce the same result.

## Shape the Groove

Turn the six macro knobs to reshape the feel:

- **Complexity** — how many lanes are active and how intricate their patterns are
- **Density** — how many hits per cycle
- **Syncopation** — how far onsets shift off the grid
- **Swing** — shuffle feel, from straight to deep swing
- **Tension** — dissonance and rhythmic friction between lanes
- **Humanize** — timing and velocity variation

Each knob influences multiple engine parameters at once. Start with **Density** and **Swing** — they have the most immediate impact on the feel.
